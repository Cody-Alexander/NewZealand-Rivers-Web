About the Clutha River

The Clutha River, or Mata-Au, is the second longest river in New Zealand. Known for its vibrant waters and powerful flow, the Clutha is one of the country’s most picturesque and essential rivers.

Beauty

The Clutha River is renowned for its striking turquoise waters that contrast beautifully against the surrounding landscapes. The river’s flow is both powerful and serene, offering breathtaking views as it winds through Central Otago. Visitors are often captivated by the natural beauty of the river, making it a popular spot for photographers, nature enthusiasts, and adventurers alike.

Culture

For the local Māori, the Clutha River is known as Mata-Au and is deeply tied to their history and traditions. The river served as a significant resource for food, transport, and connection between communities. Today, it continues to hold cultural significance, symbolizing both the natural strength and the heritage of the Otago region.

History

The Clutha River played a critical role in the history of the Otago region, particularly during the gold rush of the 1860s. The river’s powerful flow was used for gold mining, and towns such as Clyde and Alexandra grew along its banks. Today, hydroelectric dams along the river contribute significantly to New Zealand’s power supply, making it an essential part of the country’s infrastructure.
